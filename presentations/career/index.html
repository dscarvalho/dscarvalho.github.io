Current research

Patent claim segmentation

  • Identifying roles that characterize a part of an idea described in a patent claim and tagging such parts so they can be quickly analyzed and compared.
  • Semantic representation of a claim segment is challenging, since it involves different domains of knowledge and writing practices.
  • Interpretability of the claims (specially cross-language) is of special interest for healthcare institutions.
  • Faster and reliable access to such information can considerably speed-up drug and vaccine development in underdeveloped countries.
  • Interpretability of the claims (specially cross-language) is of special interest for healthcare institutions.
  • Faster and reliable access to such information can considerably speed-up drug and vaccine development in underdeveloped countries.
As for my current research, I have been working on the problem of patent claim segmentation, which allows us to identify roles that characterize parts of the ideas described in patent claims, so we can compare and search them more efficiently. Special attention is given to the ability to interpret the meaning of such claim segments, as they carry key information to accelerate drug and vaccine development in underdeveloped countries.

Patent claim segmentation

Approach: sequence tagging of claim sentences (BiLSTM + CRF).

Multiple levels of encoding (character, morpheme, word) for different features.

Learning from manually annotated claims, based on professional analysis and advice (pattent attorney).

The approach adopted in this case was the tagging of encoded sentences via BiLSTM combined with CRF, where the features are encoded in multiple levels. The training was done supervised on claims annotated manually with expert advice.

Patent claim segmentation

  • Performance evaluation is done in two stages:
  • Initially by testing for tagging accuracy on manually annotated input (0.74 F1 so far).
  • Then, performing classification by the patents' international classification code (IPC) hierarchy (pending, ~0.668 for the state-of-the-art at subclass level [BERT]).
And the evaluation is done in two stages: the first by testing the tagging accuracy with regards to the annotated data, which showed some promising results, and then by using the segments' information to help classification of patents by IPC hierarchy, which is pending.

Bibliographic analysis

  • Approach: The network analysis involves the NLP tasks of topic modeling and named entity disambiguation.
  • Also involves graph pattern identification.
  • Success can be measured by the amount of statistically significant information that can be obtained from graph distances (e.g., betweenness centrality, eccentricity) and topic cluster distances.
The approach to this analysis is centered around exploiting connections among research topics, researchers, and their affiliations. This involves both NLP tasks, like topic modeling and entity disambiguation, and also identification of graph patterns. The success is going to be measured in terms of the statistically significant information obteined from graph and topic cluster distances.

Online Social Media Literacy

  • Fake news and misinformation online have become a type of 'plague' in modern information society.
  • Teaching people how to identify misleading social media content by themselves is a large step to attenuate the problem, but quite challenging.
  • Social Media Literacy: Enabling people to properly contextualize and interpret the social media they consume.
  • Based on text content only or eye-searchable characteristics (#connections, recent post rate, among others).
Another one of my recent topics of study is the application of Explainable AI to tackle online social media literacy. The idea is to enable people to spot misinformation online, in a way that could be automatically updated to the latest trends, based only on human observalble properties of the communications.

Online Social Media Literacy

  • Approach: Bot classification that can explain the classification criteria to the user.
  • Initial use of decision trees: tradeoff of accuracy to interpretability.
  • Evaluation: Small scale experiment with different social groups showed significant difference in bot classification accuracy between the trained and untrained groups.
  • More work in progress.
This work was started with a Twitter bot classifier that explained its decision criteria to the user, based only on textual features. The option in this case was to use a simple decision tree, from which my main contribution was to create a method for translating the decision paths into human friendly sentences. Evaluation was done in a small scale experiment (< people) that compared the bot classification performance of people who used the classifier's explanations for a set time and the people who didn't, indicating a significant difference.

Drug discovery

Finding good candidates for compounds targeting specific proteins (cancer treatment) is a specially difficult task, considering the large number of possible compounds.

Using physical, chemical and pharmacokinetic properties (tested or QSAR) can lead to valuable insights, but the search space is still quite big.

Approach: A combination of different Machine Learning methods, curated drug data and database indexing techniques might be what is needed to reach candidate sets small enough to go directly to molecular simulation.

Evaluation: Precision, Recall and F1 scores of the classified sets over the curated test data. With an initial F1 of ~0.65 with a trivial classifier on a limited set, we believe it is possible to reach a precision over 90% by improving filtering processes on the full ~2 billion compound dataset.

Finally, I have been involved with automating the search for good candidate compounds to target specific proteins for cancer treatment, which is something being chased by many research groups around the world. But our team is betting on a combination of machine learning methods, curated drug data, and database indexing techniques to reduce the search space enough to make molecular simulations feasible. We are aimimg for a 90% precision score on a test set after improved filtering to be progressively strict and reliable.

Resource highlights

EasyESA

  • EasyESA: optimised infrastructure for Explicit Semantic Analysis.
  • Used as a more interpretable alternative to word2vec and other latent methods.
  • Lacks coverage of simple concepts, not included on Wikipedia.
  • https://github.com/dscarvalho/easyesa
For some resource highlights, I can mention the work on the optimised infrastructure for ESA, called EasyESA. It is known as a more interpretable alternative to latent methods, such as word2vec, and thus still attracts attention from researchers looking into XAI options, due to being highly accessible and portable.

Graphia: a rule-based SDG extractor

  • Obtaining Structured Discourse Graphs (SDGs) from factual unstructured texts.
  • Extraction is done during traversal of constituency tree, along with NER and correference resolution.
  • SDGs can be serialized as entity-centric RDF graphs.
  • http://graphia.dcc.ufrj.br
One of my first works on NLP, the Graphia rule-based SDG extractor is quite robust for the task of obtaining entity-centric graphs from factual sentences, with some types of context, by using constituency trees and a combination of LOD tools in a well integrated pipeline, which has been operating for almost a decade.

WikTDV

  • Term Definition Vectors (TDV): high dimensional, sparse vector representation of lexical items (terms) ranging from morphemes to phrases.
  • Based on the definitions of their meanings as presented in Wiktionary.
  • A highly interpretable alternative to latent methods, but needs extra work for using with NNs.
  • https://github.com/dscarvalho/tdv
Fast-forward to the trail of my PhD work, I also developed an even more interpretable alternative to latent semantic representations, based on Wiktionary term definitions, called WikTDV. It works natively with the same vector space for multiple languages, but needs some extra work for appying with NNs, due to its high sparsity.
